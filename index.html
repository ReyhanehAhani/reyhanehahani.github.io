About

Background

Hello — I’m a Master’s student in Computer Engineering at Simon Fraser University, and a Big Data Analyst with the SFU Big Data Hub, where I contribute to public-sector analytics (for example, integrating mobility, weather, and GIS for resource planning). On the research side, I work on multimodal depth estimation and on retrieval-augmented LLM pipelines for long-form, structured text.

Technically, I’m comfortable from feature stores and ETL (e.g. Spark, lake-style storage, alignment of irregular time series) through classical and hierarchical models (e.g. GLMMs, spatial covariates, leave-one-unit-out validation) to modern deep learning (PyTorch/JAX, ViT/CLIP-style encoders, FiLM and adapter-style conditioning) and LLM application engineering (LangGraph, chunking for citations, schema-constrained outputs, evals that stress grounding). I tend to keep assumptions explicit, prefer reproducible notebooks and tracked experiments, and use ablations when the question is genuinely “what moved the metric?”

I’m especially interested in retrieval-heavy LLM systems (legal and enterprise text, multi-hop QA, tool-augmented agents), efficient adaptation of VLMs (LoRA, adapters, selective fine-tuning), language as a control signal in vision, and applied settings where spatial or temporal structure matters. I also value work that connects to policy or operations—models are most satisfying when someone can act on the output.

Outside the desk, I swim regularly, enjoy origami and hiking, and I like picking up languages: Farsi, Turkish, Azerbaijani, and English are comfortable; I’m still improving French.
